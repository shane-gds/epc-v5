# Project Vision: "The Green Detective" (EPC v4)
This project merges UK public housing and transaction records to help green-energy retrofitters, social housing providers, and private landlords identify properties failing the UK’s Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards (MEES)[cite: 1]. Under MEES, private rented homes must reach EPC Band C by October 1, 2030, or face fines up to £30,000[cite: 1]. 

The "Green Detective" provides the exact coordinate mapping, physical property attributes, and corporate ownership trees to enable localized, street-by-street retrofitting campaigns[cite: 1].

### The Technology Stack:
- **Core Engine:** dbt + DuckDB (running on a Beelink SER8 mini PC in a Proxmox VM).
- **Matching Engine:** Splink (unsupervised/supervised probabilistic record linkage based on Fellegi-Sunter math).
- **Target Visualization/Storage:** Neo4j (Graph Database for network and spatial intelligence).

# Data Factory Architecture (The 6 Stations)

The 183.7-million-row pipeline is organized into a six-station conveyor belt to push heavy math down to SQL:

### Station 1: The Intake Harbor (Bronze Layer)
- **Role:** Loads raw, untouched CSV files (HM Land Registry PPD, domestic EPC registers, ONSUD postcode directory, and geography references)[cite: 3].
- **Scale:** 183.75 million raw rows[cite: 3].

### Station 2: The Scrubbing Station (Silver Layer)
- **Role:** Sanitizes and normalizes addresses and postcodes using dbt macros[cite: 2, 3].
  - `normalise_uk_postcode`: Standardizes spacing and casing (e.g., `SW1A 1AA`)[cite: 2].
  - `normalise_address`: Blends address parts into lowercased, punctuation-free strings (e.g., `12 main street`)[cite: 2].
  - Extracts numeric low/high GBP values from messy recommendation cost text (e.g., `RANGE_PARSED`, `SINGLE_VALUE_PARSED`)[cite: 2, 3].
  - Flags impossible dates (e.g., inspections recorded after certificate lodgement)[cite: 3].

### Station 3: The GPS Mapping Lounge (Core Location Cache)
- **Role:** Maps properties to locations by matching UPRNs to the ONSUD directory[cite: 1, 3].
- **Coordinates:** 16.2 million distinct BNG (British National Grid) points are transformed to global WGS84 Latitude/Longitude once and cached, saving massive CPU[cite: 3].

### Station 4: The Speed Matchmaker (Identity Blocking)
- **Role:** Restricts comparison candidate pairs to avoid a trillion-row $O(N^2)$ Cartesian explosion[cite: 1, 3].
- **Rules:**
  - `D01_EXACT_UPRN`: Matches on exact UPRNs[cite: 3].
  - `P01_POSTCODE_PREMISE_EXACT`: Matches on identical postcode and normalized premise address strings[cite: 3].
  - `P02_SECTOR_PREMISE_EXACT`: Same sector & premise but differing postcodes to recover clerical shifts[cite: 3].

### Station 5: The Detective's Scoring Office (Splink Math Engine)
- **Role:** Runs unsupervised EM math to score similarity[cite: 1, 3].
- **Scale:** Evaluates 26,301,482 candidate pairs[cite: 3].
- **Findings:** Over 99.9% of pairs score as high-confidence (weight > 4.0), proving the blocking rules are incredibly precise[cite: 3].
- **Weights:** Postcode matching adds ~1,010,000x match likelihood; premise address adds ~837,000x; UPRN adds ~320,000x (treated as evidence, not absolute proof, due to multi-unit buildings sharing UPRNs)[cite: 3].

### Station 6: The Executive Decision Room (Assignments & Clusters)
- **Role:** Applies decision policies to assign records to golden entity UUIDs[cite: 3].
- **Current State:** Because manual labels are pending, the engine "fails closed"—writing all 54.8 million current assignments to the ledger as `UNRESOLVED` (Review Required) or `SINGLETON_NO_CANDIDATE`[cite: 3].

# Domain-Specific Gotchas (PPD vs. EPC)
When matching Land Registry PPD to DLUHC EPC data, keep three major rules in mind:
1. **Address Discrepancies (PAON/SAON):** PPD splits addresses into Primary (PAON - house numbers) and Secondary (SAON - flat numbers), while EPC lumps these into `address1`[cite: 1]. Address comparisons must use a custom-built, source-neutral premise comparator[cite: 3].
2. **The Temporal "Before" Constraint:** You must only pair a transaction with the latest EPC certificate generated *before or on* the sale date[cite: 1]. Matching to a later EPC rating is invalid, as the new owner may have already upgraded the property[cite: 1].
3. **UPRN is Not Identity:** Over 523,000 UPRNs in the UK contain multiple distinct properties (e.g., flats)[cite: 3]. UPRN matches are valuable clues but cannot automatically define a unique property entity without address checking[cite: 1, 3].

# The Commercial & Graph Edge (Neo4j Target)
The final stage of this project pivots from flat relational queries to a **Property-Corporate Network Graph**[cite: 1].

### Target Graph Schema:
`(Company: Landlord) -[:DIRECTOR_OF/OWNS]-> (Person: Director) -[:DIRECTOR_OF]-> (Company: Parent) -[:OWNED_BY]-> (Company: Subsidiary) -[:OWNS]-> (U: UPRN) -[:HAS_EPC]-> (E: EPC_Rating)`[cite: 1]

### Commercial Value Angles:
1. **Unmasking SPV Shell Companies:** Landlords register individual Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) per building[cite: 1]. By linking Companies House data in Neo4j, we can traverse common directors/addresses to reveal massive, multi-property corporate portfolios[cite: 1].
2. **Spatial Aggregation (Louvain Community Detection):** Finds natural "hotspots" of highly inefficient housing where a contractor can mobilize and insulate 20 adjacent homes at once[cite: 1].
3. **MEES Liability Auditing:** Traverses the graph to calculate a lender's or landlord's total compliance remediation cost before the 2030 deadline[cite: 1].
4. **GDPR Strategy:** Address data is technically personal data under UK GDPR despite Open Government Licences (OGL v3)[cite: 1]. We remain compliant B2B under Legitimate Interest (supporting the Net Zero strategy) and keep data aggregated/attribute-pure (UPRN, wall type, EPC rating) without storing resident names[cite: 1].

# Operational Performance Rules (DuckDB/VM Tuning)
- **OOM Protection:** Aggregations on millions of rows are restricted to single-threaded execution (`EPC_V4_DUCKDB_THREADS=1`) with an 8 GB memory limit to prevent Proxmox VM memory-exhaustion crashes[cite: 3].
- **Late Materialization:** To avoid sorting wide, text-heavy tables in memory, queries sort lightweight keys first (e.g., `ORDER BY match_weight DESC LIMIT 20`) or filter on confidence (`WHERE match_weight > 4.0`) before joining address strings[cite: 3].
